Pronunciation

Vowels
a = ah, i = ee, u = oo, e = eh, o = oh
Examples: ka = kah, su = soo, ne = neh, to = toh
R
Between English r/l/d. Very light.
Double consonant
Small pause: kitte = “kit-te”
Long vowels
ō / ū are held slightly longer.
u / desu
Often very soft: desu ≈ dess
Polite safe ending
…desu / …masu
You do not need perfect pronunciation. Slow, polite, and clear works well.

Tiny Etiquette Notes

  • Polite default: sumimasen + onegaishimasu + arigatō gozaimasu.
  • Pointing at menu items is normal and practical.
  • Speak calmly; volume matters more than accent.
  • Cash still helps, though cards are common in many places.
  • Queueing and quiet public behavior matter.
